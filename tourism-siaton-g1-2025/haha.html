About the Municipality

Siaton is a bustling coastal municipality situated in the southern part of Negros Oriental, Philippines. It holds the distinction of being the province's southernmost town.

Known for its rich history and strategic location, Siaton serves as a prime eco-tourism destination. Travelers are drawn to its contrasting landscapes, from the clear waters of its hidden bays to the verdant peaks of its mountains. We are proud of our warm community and vibrant local culture, making it a perfect spot for authentic Filipino exploration.

Local Food Specialties

Tinolang Isda

Fish soup and is a popular breakfast favorite, especially in coastal areas like Siaton where fresh fish is readily available from local fishermen.

Kinilaw (Ceviche)

Fresh seafood cured in vinegar, ginger, onions, and local spices. Known for its refreshing and vibrant flavor—perfect for coastal dining.

Sinugba

Thanks to its coastal location, Siaton offers an abundance of freshly caught fish, squid, and shellfish—often served grilled and paired with native sauces.

Fiestas & Major Events

Experience the vibrant culture and community spirit of Siaton through its annual celebrations.

Inagta Festival

Date: December 5

A vibrant cultural and culinary celebration showcasing Inagta Festival, a cultural and religious celebration unique to the municipality of Siaton, Negros Oriental, Philippines. It honors the town's patron saint and celebrates local history and indigenous heritage.

Siaton Town Fiesta

Date: December 8

Siaton Town Fiesta is a lively event featuring parades, food stalls and religious activities.
